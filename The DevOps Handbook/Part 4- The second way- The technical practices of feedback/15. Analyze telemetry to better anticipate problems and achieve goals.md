### Use of Gaussian curves
One of the ways one can use to analyze a production metric is computing the mean and standard deviations to for a Gaussian curve. If there is anything abnormal coming from the output, it should be treated as a problem.
### Analyzing the past to predict the future
Determine what to monitor by looking at the past incidences. This makes it simple for you to choose what to monitor and prevent those incidences from happening in the future. 
### Monitoring metrics that are not Gaussian
Without Gaussian metrics, we can leverage past metrics to identify anomalies and use that data to predict potential anomalies. The beauty of this is that one can leverage everyday data analysis tools such as Tableau or be innovative like Netflix and develop a service that addresses [their problem](https://netflixtechblog.com/scryer-netflixs-predictive-auto-scaling-engine-a3f8fc922270).
