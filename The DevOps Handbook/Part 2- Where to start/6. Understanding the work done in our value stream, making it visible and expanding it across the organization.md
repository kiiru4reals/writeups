### [Value stream mapping at Nordstrom]([[5. Selecting what valuestream to start with#Where did Nordstrom start]])
Nordstrom learnt that before such a change, it is important to involve everyone at the beginning to do a [value stream mapping exercise]([[6. Understanding the work done in our value stream, making it visible and expanding it across the organization#Value Stream mapping]]).
### Getting your stakeholders in one room
After identifying an application that can be 'DevOpsed', you are required to get all your stakeholders together so as to understand how the entire value stream works. The team members may include:
1. Product owner
2. Developer(s)
3. Quality Assurance
4. Site Reliability Engineers (SRE)
5. Cyber security
6. Release managers
7. Technical executives
### Value Stream mapping
Identifying your value streams starts with understanding the SDLC, so as to see where the bottlenecks are. The purpose of this exercise is to identify where things take too long or where significant rework is involved. The next step is to document the entire process with [the time taken and effective delivery rate]([[1. Agile, Continous Delivery and the three ways]]).
### Creating a team
When creating a transformation team, it is recommended to create a team that is ready to make disruptive changes, free them from their responsibilities, select generalists and not specialists and teams with good relations with key areas of the organization.
### Setting goals
Agree on a goal(or a set of goals) that can be achieved within a span of six months to two years. Based on this goal, break down the goal to small chunks that can be achieved in multiple iterations.
### Room for technical debt
Spend 20% of your time managing technical debt and non-functional requirements to prevent cases of getting to a situation where you are constantly doing patch work and fire fighting.
