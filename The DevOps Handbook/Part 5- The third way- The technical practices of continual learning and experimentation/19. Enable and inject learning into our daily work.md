### Mistakes ==  Knowledge
Look at mistakes as areas of learning and knowledge gaps. This makes it possible for accountability to be taken by responsible teams which leads to the dissipation of knowledge across the organization. Conducting blameless post-postmortems makes it possible for teams to perform more mistakes and faster growth within the organization.
### Have retrospectives after incidences
Have retrospectives after an incident has been fixed to ensure that teams learn from it, as part of the retrospectives lessons learnt should be documented and action items assigned to the relevant stakeholders. Share these retrospective reports to as many people as possible to promote ability to learn from the incident and for organizational memory to prevent repetition and make future incidences easier to resolve.
### Making chaos your friend
Work on introducing chaos into your systems continuously so as to identify points of weakness and to build a [pattern of resilience][[4. Principles of continuous learning and experimentation#Building resilience patterns]].
### Game-day exercises
Prepare for the worst by having game day exercises. To prevent exposing teams and creating a blame culture inform them of this exercise beforehand so that they can work on improving the faults they have identified, once this is done proceed to execute malicious scenarios so as to assess the team's readiness in case of unprecedented issues and to get learning points.
